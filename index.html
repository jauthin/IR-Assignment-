ROUGE Evaluation Recall-Oriented Understudy for Gisting Evaluation
What is ROUGE? A family of metrics for evaluating automatic summarization by comparing a generated summary against the original text used as a reference.

ROUGE-1 measures unigram (single word) overlap — how many individual words from the original appear in the summary.
ROUGE-2 measures bigram (consecutive word pair) overlap — captures phrasing and fluency retention.
ROUGE-L uses the Longest Common Subsequence (LCS) — captures sentence-level structure without requiring consecutive matches.

Each variant reports Precision (what fraction of the summary's grams appear in the original), Recall (what fraction of the original's grams are covered), and F1 (harmonic mean). Note: extractive summaries naturally score high because sentences are lifted verbatim — ROUGE is most meaningful when comparing different summarization approaches.
